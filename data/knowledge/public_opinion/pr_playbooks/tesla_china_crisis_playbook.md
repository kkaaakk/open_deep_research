# Tesla China Crisis Response Playbook — CONFIDENTIAL

## Purpose
This playbook governs Tesla China's response to public-opinion incidents involving vehicle safety, quality complaints, regulatory inquiries, and social media crises. Must be reviewed by Legal + PR before any external communication referencing this document.

## Response Principles (Mandatory)

1. **Acknowledge first, explain later.** Every public response must begin by acknowledging the customer's concern before offering technical context.
2. **Never speculate before inspection.** Do not state a root cause, deny a defect, or attribute blame until Service + Engineering complete vehicle diagnosis.
3. **Separate facts from claims.** Public statements must clearly distinguish: (a) confirmed facts, (b) claims under investigation, (c) misinformation to correct.
4. **Privacy is absolute.** Never disclose owner name, VIN, phone, plate number, service records, or location in any public communication.
5. **Provide a clear next step.** Every response must end with a concrete action for the customer: service appointment booking link, hotline number, case owner assignment.
6. **Safety/recall mentions trigger escalation.** Any public discussion involving "safety", "recall", "fire", or "injury" requires Legal + Quality + After-Sales + PR review before publication.

## Escalation Matrix

| Trigger | Level | Response Time |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Single social media complaint, no safety claim | Routine | 24h acknowledgment |
| Same issue appears on 2+ platforms within 48h | High | 4h initial response, 24h detailed |
| 3+ complaints on 12315/12365 about same product issue | High | 4h cross-functional review |
| Regulator (SAMR/MIIT) names Tesla publicly | Critical | 1h CEO briefing, 2h public response |
| Official media (Xinhua/CCTV/Guangming) publishes negative report | Critical | 1h CEO briefing, 3h holding statement |
| Unverified recall claims trending on Weibo top 50 | Critical | 2h verification + holding statement |

## Holding Statement Templates

### Template A — Product Quality Complaint (non-safety)
"Tesla has taken note of [Owner X]'s experience shared on [Platform]. Our Service team has contacted the owner to arrange a complimentary vehicle inspection at their preferred Service Center. We take every customer concern seriously and will provide a transparent diagnosis. Until the inspection is complete, we cannot confirm the specific cause. We will share verified findings with the owner directly."

### Template B — Safety-Related Incident
"Tesla is aware of reports regarding [incident description]. Vehicle safety is our highest priority. Our engineering and service teams are working urgently to investigate. We have contacted the owner and relevant authorities. We will provide verified updates through official Tesla channels. We urge the public to await official investigation results before drawing conclusions."

### Template C — Regulatory/Rumor Response
"Tesla China has noted recent online discussions regarding [topic]. We state clearly: [confirmed fact / denial]. Tesla complies fully with all applicable Chinese laws and regulations. We welcome scrutiny of our products and processes through official channels. We reserve the right to take legal action against those who fabricate and spread false information."
