# Tesla Battery Customer FAQ — Internal Reference for Service & Support Teams

## Battery Degradation

**Q: How fast does a Tesla battery degrade?**
Data from our global fleet shows average capacity retention of ~92% at 100,000 miles (~160,000 km) across all chemistries. LFP packs typically show less degradation than NCA/NCM over equivalent mileage. Individual variation depends on charging habits, climate, and mileage.

**Q: What is considered "normal" battery degradation?**
Up to ~10% capacity loss in the first 100,000 miles is within the normal range. Degradation is typically non-linear: faster in the first 20,000-30,000 miles, then stabilizes. If a customer reports >15% loss within warranty, escalate to Engineering for BMS diagnostic review.

**Q: Does frequent Supercharging damage the battery?**
Occasional Supercharging has minimal impact. Regular exclusive Supercharging (>80% of sessions) on NCA packs may accelerate degradation by 2-5% over 100,000 miles compared to Level 2 charging. LFP packs are less affected by DC fast charging. The BMS automatically manages charging power to protect battery health.

## Range Display

**Q: Why did my displayed range drop after an OTA update?**
Displayed range is a BMS estimate based on voltage measurement, temperature, and historical driving data. OTA updates that refine the estimation algorithm may cause the displayed number to change. This reflects improved estimation accuracy, NOT physical capacity loss. If the customer insists range loss is real, schedule a battery health check at a Service Center.

**Q: Does Tesla "lock" battery capacity via software?**
No. Tesla does not artificially limit battery capacity. All OTA updates that touch BMS parameters are designed to protect cell longevity or improve estimation accuracy. Any change that reduces usable capacity would trigger warranty obligations.

## Cold Weather Performance

**Q: Why does my range drop significantly in winter?**
Cold weather affects all EVs. At -10°C, expect 20-30% range reduction. At -20°C, 30-40% reduction. This is temporary — range recovers when temperatures rise. Recommendations:
- Pre-condition the vehicle while plugged in before departure
- Use Scheduled Departure to warm the battery
- Keep the vehicle plugged in when parked in extreme cold

**Q: Why does LFP lose more range in cold weather?**
LFP chemistry has inherently higher internal resistance at low temperatures. The apparent range loss is more noticeable than NCA, but the underlying chemical degradation is actually lower. The displayed range will recover fully when temperatures normalize.

## Charging

**Q: Why did Supercharging speed slow down on my older vehicle?**
After extensive DC fast charging (typically >500 sessions on NCA packs), the BMS may slightly reduce peak charging power to manage cell aging. This is designed to extend battery life. The reduction is typically 5-10% and is documented in the owner's manual.

**Q: Should I charge my LFP battery to 100%?**
Yes. Tesla recommends charging LFP-equipped vehicles to 100% at least once per week. This is necessary for BMS calibration to maintain accurate range estimation. Unlike NCA, LFP does not experience accelerated degradation at high SOC.

## Warranty & Replacement

**Q: What does the battery warranty cover?**
8 years, mileage varies by model. Guarantees minimum 70% capacity retention. Covers manufacturing defects and abnormal degradation. Does NOT cover: damage from accidents, flooding, improper storage (discharged to 0% for extended periods), or unauthorized modifications.

**Q: How much does a battery replacement cost out of warranty?**
Costs vary by model and region. US market: approximately $13,000-$21,000 depending on pack size. China market: approximately ¥127,000-¥247,000. Self-paid replacements include a 4-year/50,000-mile warranty on the new pack.

**Q: Will I get a new or remanufactured battery for a warranty replacement?**
Tesla may use remanufactured battery packs that meet or exceed 70% of original capacity. Remanufactured packs undergo the same validation testing as new packs and carry the remainder of the original warranty period.

## Safety

**Q: Are Tesla batteries safe?**
Yes. Tesla's battery packs are designed with multiple layers of protection: cell-level fusing, liquid thermal management, reinforced pack enclosure, and BMS monitoring 24/7. Tesla vehicle fire rates are approximately 1/10th the rate of ICE vehicle fires per mile driven. GB38031-2025 certification applies to all China-market vehicles produced after 2026-01-01.

**Q: What should I do if I suspect a battery issue?**
1. If you see a warning on the touchscreen, follow the on-screen instructions.
2. If you hear unusual noises from the battery area or smell burning, pull over safely, exit the vehicle, and contact Tesla Roadside Assistance immediately.
3. For non-urgent concerns (e.g., unexpected range loss), schedule a Service appointment via the Tesla App.
4. Do not attempt to inspect or repair the battery yourself.
